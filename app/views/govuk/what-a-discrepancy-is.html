What a discrepancy is

A discrepancy is when the information that an obliged entity holds about a beneficial owner clearly shows that the information recorded by Companies House, in terms of the definition of a PSC, is incorrect.

A discrepancy exists when a relevant entity has information that clearly indicates that the PSC information recorded by Companies House is inaccurate.

This could include a discrepancy with a:

  • person listed as a PSC
  • missing PSC
  • PSC exemption
  • PSC type
  • address
  • place of registration
  • date of birth
  • nationality
  • legal form
  • company statement

Our focus is on factual errors, such as a missing PSC or an incorrect service address, not typing mistakes. It is not a discrepancy when the entity holds information that we do not include on the PSC register.

A spelling error is not a discrepancy. For example, Jon Smith instead of John Smith, or a missing or slightly different spelling of a middle name.

Obliged entities can advise the company to contact us to correct these minor errors.

If a beneficial owner’s correspondence address is different from the service address on the PSC register

The address shown for a PSC on Companies House records is a service address - this does not have to be their home address. Obliged entities should take this into consideration.

PSCs must provide their home address to Companies House, but it’s kept on a private register. It’s only available on request to credit reference agencies and specified public authorities.

As the PSC’s home address is not available on the public register, it might not be a discrepancy if it does not match the address given to an obliged entity. If the address a client has provided is different to what is shown on the register, you may wish to check with them if they have used an alternative to their home address as a service address.

You should only make a report when there is a clear discrepancy between the same types of address.
