What a discrepancy is

A discrepancy is when the information that an obliged entity holds about a beneficial owner clearly shows that the information recorded by Companies House is incorrect. Our focus is on factual errors, such as a missing PSC or an incorrect service address, not typing mistakes.

A discrepancy exists when a relevant entity has information that clearly indicates that the PSC information recorded by Companies House is inaccurate.

This could include a discrepancy with a:

  • person listed as a PSC
  • missing PSC
  • PSC exemption
  • PSC type
  • address
  • place of registration
  • date of birth
  • legal form
  • company statement

It is not a discrepancy when the entity holds information that goes beyond or is not required for the PSC register.

A spelling error is not a discrepancy. For example, Jon Smith instead of John Smith, or a missing or slightly different spelling of a middle name.

Obliged entities should tell the company to contact us to correct these minor errors.

If a beneficial owner’s correspondence address is different from the service address on the PSC register

The address shown for a PSC on Companies House records is a service address - this does not have to be their home address. Obliged entities should take this into consideration.

PSCs must provide their home address to Companies House, but it’s kept on a private register. It’s only available on request to credit reference agencies and specified public authorities.

As the PSC’s home address is not available on the public register, it might not be a discrepancy if it does not match the address given to an obliged entity.

Obliged entities should check with their client if the correspondence address they’ve given when a new business relationship is being set up has also been given as the PSC’s usual residential address (home address) with Companies House.

If they confirm it’s the same address, you do not need to make a discrepancy report.
